What is a Unix timestamp?

A Unix timestamp counts elapsed time since 00:00:00 UTC on 1 January 1970. The number is timezone neutral; the readable date changes only when you display it in a timezone.

What happens on 19 January 2038?

Older 32-bit signed timestamp systems overflow at 03:14:07 UTC on 19 January 2038. Modern 64-bit systems have a much larger range.
